Where captures go

Rules for particular sites

The most specific rule wins, so scholar.google.com can behave differently from google.com. A rule removes a decision from every capture on that site.

What gets captured

Highlight on pages

Select text on a page and a toolbar appears: six colours, highlight or underline, a note if you want one. Highlights are painted again whenever you revisit the page, land in the page's row, and — when the page has a dedicated note — under its Annotations heading. Click any highlight to recolour, restyle, copy, note, or remove it. The toolbar starts from whatever colour and style you used last. Needs permission to read pages, asked for when you turn this on.

The sidebar

One tick: this asks for the page-reading permission the sidebar needs (the popup borrows it from the toolbar click; a sidebar has no click to borrow from) and confirms everything is ready.

Ready. To open it:

  • Firefox: View → Sidebar → Knowledge Views, or Ctrl+B and pick it from the sidebar dropdown.
  • Chrome/Edge: click the side-panel icon in the toolbar and choose Knowledge Views.

The sidebar stays open while you browse: your views, row editing, and search without switching windows.

How it opens

The sidebar is the fuller surface: it stays open while you browse and adds your views, so you can work through a reading queue, edit rows and search without switching applications. Open it from your browser's sidebar control — in Firefox, View → Sidebar → Knowledge Views.

Show what you already have

Off by default. Turning it on means every page you visit is checked against your vault. That check never leaves this computer, but it is still a thing worth choosing rather than assuming. It also needs Allow searching or Allow reading to be on in Obsidian.

Mark search results

Off by default. Turning it on asks for access to search sites — Google, Scholar, DuckDuckGo, Bing, Brave, arXiv, PubMed and Semantic Scholar — in one go, rather than one at a time. Only the addresses of results are checked, and only against your own vault; nothing about what you have is sent back to the page beyond which links it recognised.
